Relevant Work Experience

Harris, Haunted (album) — music producer — 06/2017-present

Contact: Eric Chipman [email: ericchipman@gmail.com] [phone: ]

Description: I've been producing albums since I was 17, but this is the first project I'd really call a "job," both in terms of its scope and its budget. Production means wearing many hats, but the most surprising new role this time around has that of a cheerleader, keeping everyone excited about the big picture. This was vital both in the early phases, when that picture was difficult to see, and especially now as we push through the long polish stage. I've learned that whether it's a singer struggling with a part, a songwriter frustrated by an inability to explain their vision, or a mixer wanting to cut corners, I'm fairly good at facillitating direct & open communication between team members, and encouraging an environment of pointed experimentation, productive failures, and celebrated successes.

American English Academy — ESL teacher — 10/2016-present

Contact: Monica Mauro [email: monica@aec.edu] [phone: ]

Description: My current dayjob is teaching small ESL classes to college-bound foreign students. The most important skills I've developed as an ESL teacher are: lesson planning & structure (I've found this applies well to many meeting-like situations), excellence in spoken & written communication, patience, and the ability to simplify concepts to varying degrees of complexity for different audiences.

Giant Frame — co-founder, composer — 2014-present

Description: Giant Frame makes music for film. In addition to music, my job includes communicating with clients, writing contracts and invoices, managing a small business, finding creative excitement within the generative bounds of other people's constraints.

Great Harvest Bakery — baker, kneader, & production manager —

Description: At the bakery, I managed a small team of kneaders and bakers. In that environment, time is critical, and it was my job to keep the team working smoothly and productively, while maintaining a fun environment. That meant having enough understanding of each person's job that I could jump in and help where needed, while communicating needs and expectations within and between the smaller teams.

Education

University of Utah — English BA, Environmental Studies BS — 2009-2012

In my English BA, I learned to write with clarity, to read text & media deeply, to critique through many lenses, and to understand the role that a text's form plays in the way it makes meaning. I learned that while cynicism is easy and feels powerful, joy is contagious and contains actual power.

My Environmental Studies BS was an interdisciplinary degree, which required me to take classes from a wide variety of departments. I learned how cells divide; the circle geometry of river systems; a love of rocks; a curiosity about processes; environmental ethics and feminist economics;

I was an extremely dedicated student, graduating summa cum laude in both my degrees.
